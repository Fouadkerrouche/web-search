Digital Mental Health Interventions: Evidence-Based Outcomes

JAMA Psychiatry | January 2025

JAMA Psychiatry reports on large-scale study examining digital therapy platforms. Research demonstrates comparable effectiveness to traditional therapy for mild to moderate depression and anxiety, with improved accessibility and reduced costs.

The randomized controlled trial included 12,000 participants across eight countries. App-based cognitive behavioral therapy showed 68% symptom reduction, with treatment adherence rates 40% higher than in-person sessions due to convenience and anonymity factors.
