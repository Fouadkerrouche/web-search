Enhanced CRISPR Systems Achieve Single-Base Precision

Cell | Volume 188, Issue 3

Cell journal publishes breakthrough in CRISPR technology enabling single-nucleotide editing with 99.9% accuracy. Research team develops new guide RNA designs eliminating off-target effects, accelerating gene therapy development for genetic diseases.

Prime editing 2.0 demonstrates unprecedented precision in correcting disease-causing mutations. The system successfully treats sickle cell disease and beta-thalassemia in clinical trials, with patients showing complete remission and normal hemoglobin production after six months.
