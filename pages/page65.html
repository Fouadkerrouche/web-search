Potentially Habitable Exoplanet Detected 47 Light-Years Away

The Astrophysical Journal | Vol. 962, No. 1

The Astrophysical Journal announces discovery of Earth-sized exoplanet in habitable zone of red dwarf star. Spectroscopic analysis reveals atmospheric composition including water vapor and oxygen signatures, marking most promising candidate for extraterrestrial life.

TOI-8453b exhibits temperate climate with estimated surface temperature of 285K. Transit photometry and radial velocity measurements confirm rocky composition and mass 1.2 times Earth. Biosignature gases detected in atmospheric spectrum warrant follow-up observations with next-generation space telescopes.
