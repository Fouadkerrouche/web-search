Evidence-Based Learning Techniques for Enhanced Retention

Psychological Science | Vol. 36, Issue 1

Psychological Science publishes meta-analysis of learning methodologies across 150 studies. Research identifies spaced repetition, active recall, and interleaving as most effective techniques, improving long-term retention by 50% compared to traditional study methods.

Neuroimaging reveals retrieval practice strengthens neural pathways more effectively than passive review. Optimal spacing intervals follow power law distribution, with review sessions scheduled at exponentially increasing intervals. Implementation in classroom settings shows 65% improvement in exam performance.
