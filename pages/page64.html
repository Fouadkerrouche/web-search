Room-Temperature Superconductivity Achieved at Ambient Pressure

Physical Review Letters | Vol. 134, Issue 2

Physical Review Letters reports unprecedented achievement in materials science. Research team synthesizes hydrogen-rich compound exhibiting superconductivity at 294K and standard atmospheric pressure, revolutionizing potential quantum computing applications.

The carbonaceous sulfur hydride material eliminates cryogenic cooling requirements, enabling practical superconducting applications. Zero electrical resistance confirmed through magnetic susceptibility and critical current density measurements, with stability maintained across wide temperature range.
