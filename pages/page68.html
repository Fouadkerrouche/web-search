Molecular Pathways of Long-Term Memory Formation

Neuron | Vol. 112, Issue 24

Neuron journal reveals detailed mechanisms of memory consolidation at synaptic level. Research using advanced imaging techniques identifies specific protein cascades critical for converting short-term memories to permanent storage, offering therapeutic targets for Alzheimer's disease.

Two-photon microscopy reveals BDNF-TrkB signaling pathway as master regulator of synaptic plasticity. Optogenetic manipulation confirms causal role in memory formation. Novel pharmacological agents enhancing this pathway show promise in early-stage clinical trials for cognitive enhancement.
