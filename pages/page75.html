Engineered Bacteria Produce Life-Saving Drugs On-Demand

Nature Biotechnology | Vol. 43, Issue 2

Nature Biotechnology showcases engineered microorganisms capable of synthesizing complex pharmaceuticals in vivo. Synthetic biology approach enables production of insulin, antibodies, and enzymes directly in patient's gut microbiome, eliminating need for external drug administration and reducing costs by 90%.

Programmable genetic circuits respond to disease biomarkers, activating therapeutic protein production only when needed. Contained biosafety systems prevent uncontrolled proliferation. Clinical trials demonstrate stable colonization and sustained drug delivery over 12 months in Type 1 diabetes patients.
