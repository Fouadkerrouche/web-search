Effectiveness of Community-Based Health Interventions

British Medical Journal | Vol. 384

British Medical Journal publishes systematic review of preventive health programs. Meta-analysis of 200 studies shows community-led initiatives reduce chronic disease incidence by 35%, advocating for increased public health funding and grassroots approaches.

Integrated interventions addressing nutrition, physical activity, and mental health demonstrate sustained impact. Cost-benefit analysis reveals $7 savings for every dollar invested in prevention programs, with greatest effects observed in underserved communities receiving culturally-adapted interventions.
