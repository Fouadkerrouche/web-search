Graphene-Based Filtration Systems Remove 99.9% of Contaminants

Environmental Science & Technology | Vol. 59, Issue 3

Environmental Science & Technology presents innovative water purification method using graphene oxide membranes. Technology removes microplastics, heavy metals, and organic pollutants at 100x speed of conventional systems while consuming 80% less energy, enabling affordable clean water access.

Single-atom catalyst embedded in graphene framework enables selective pollutant degradation. Pilot installations in developing regions provide clean drinking water for 500,000 people at $0.02 per liter, representing 90% cost reduction. Scalable manufacturing processes facilitate global deployment.
